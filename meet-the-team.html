Meet The Team

Our Mission

Our mission is to improve the natural habitats of animals in the United Kingdom through knowledge conservation and maintenance. Since 2020 we have planted over 200 acres of trees and improved the habitat for a vast array of animals in all the UK’s national parks. We have signed up over 10,000 people to the free newsletter and had multiple petitions discussed in parliament.

We still have a long way to go. We hope to reach over 100,000 UK households by 2030. With that network of like minded people will be able to make a difference. If each household that subscribes designates a small area of their garden to natural wildlife, or help to improve a local area. We really believe we may start seeing a difference in our lives.

The Team

BWC is a charity run by 10 employees each assigned to an area of the UK and always willing to offer help and advice in your local area. Send us as a message on facebook or instagram and we’ll get back to you as soon as possible.

BWC was established by Sarah Smith in 2020 and her husband Dan Smith, two people with a passion for wildlife conservation. Sarah started by writing a local newspaper segment about nature in the community and Dan, the avid photographer, would take the photos. Very quickly members of the local community would approach Sarah and suggest projects and donations.

The Charity

In late 2020 after receiving several suggestions and some donations, Sarah decided to formally register the BWC as a charity and take her news segments and turn them into an email newsletter. Sarah and Dan continued to run the BWC alongside their day jobs until 2022 where they made the decision to retire and work as a full time volunteer with the BWC. Between 2022 and January 2024 the popularity of Sarah’s newsletter exploded and Sarah decided to hire some extra support.
